Price / troy oz — the global standard unit. Every gold product on earth is priced from this. 1 troy oz = 31.1035 grams. The USD figure underneath is the raw international price before currency conversion.
USD/MYR rate — this single number is why Malaysian gold prices move even when international gold is flat. If MYR weakens (rate rises from 4.06 → 4.20), your RM gold price rises automatically. The metal did not move — only the currency did.
Intraday high / low — the highest and lowest price today. The gap between them is today's trading range. Wide range = high volatility. Narrow range = quiet market. Compare current price to both to know where you are in today's range.
Day open — where the price started this session. Compare current price to open: above = green day, below = red day. This is your reference zero for all intraday movement.

Buy zone Set alert BELOW current price
Gold is at RM 530. You think RM 500 is a good entry. Set "falls below RM 500." When gold dips there, you get notified — a decision window to buy before it recovers. Discipline over emotion.
Stop-loss Set alert BELOW your entry price
You bought at RM 520. Set "falls below RM 495." If triggered, you're in an unrealised loss and the trend may be breaking. Not a command to sell — a signal to re-examine your thesis.
Profit target Set alert ABOVE your entry price
You bought at RM 510, target RM 560. Set "rises above RM 560." When triggered, your target is reached — take profit, or let it run, but at least you don't miss the moment while away from screen.
Alerts fire as a browser toast notification at the bottom of the screen — and as a system notification if you allow it when prompted. The badge changes to ⚡ Triggered in the list.
Section 4 of 4
The formula
The math behind every number — simple, but understanding it protects you from misreading the price.
RM per gram = ( XAU/USD ÷ 31.1035 ) × USD/MYR

Example: ( 4,056.27 ÷ 31.1035 ) × 4.0645 = RM 530.47 / gram
XAU/USD spot — international gold price in USD per troy ounce. Set by LBMA (London) and COMEX (New York). This is the global reference every gold product on earth is derived from.
÷ 31.1035 — converts troy ounces to grams. One troy ounce always equals 31.1035 grams. This is a fixed constant — it never changes.
× USD/MYR rate — converts USD to Ringgit. This is the variable that moves MYR gold prices even on quiet international gold days. Watch Bank Negara Malaysia rate announcements — they move this number.
Scenario A — MYR weakens
Gold flat at $4,056. But USD/MYR goes 4.06 → 4.20. Your RM price rises from RM 530 → RM 548/g. You pay more for the same gold. A weak Ringgit is bad for gold buyers.
Scenario B — MYR strengthens
Gold rises to $4,200. But USD/MYR drops to 3.90. The effects partially cancel. Your MYR gain is smaller than the USD gain suggests. Currency dampens your profit.
Practical rule: Always check both XAU/USD and the USD/MYR rate before deciding to buy or sell. A "rising" RM gold price could be entirely due to a weakening Ringgit — not because gold itself is in demand.
